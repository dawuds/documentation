## 1. Purpose

To set out GIBB's **Technology Audit Universe**, the **rolling 3-year audit plan**, and the **annual audit schedule** — establishing the third line's independent assurance over IT governance, technology risk, cyber resilience, business continuity, third-party risk, customer information, NCII compliance, cloud, data, and AI governance.

This plan is the **mandatory artefact** required by RMiT Section 13 and is the document a BNM examiner asks for to verify that GIBB's third line is operating with adequate coverage, risk-based prioritisation, and authority.

## 2. Scope and authority

- **Scope.** All technology-related governance, processes, and controls across GIBB and its material outsourced service providers. Includes IT, cybersecurity, business continuity, third-party / cloud, data and AI governance, NCII compliance.
- **Authority.** The Internal Audit function has unrestricted access to people, records, and systems necessary to discharge its mandate. Reports administratively to the CEO; functionally to the **Board Audit Committee** (BAC). Independence from operational and 2nd-line functions per IPPF and per RMiT Section 13.
- **Standards.** Audits are conducted in accordance with the IIA International Standards for the Professional Practice of Internal Auditing.

## 7. Co-sourcing and external assurance

Where specialist competence is required (e.g., red team, cloud security architecture, AI governance), Internal Audit co-sources with vetted external providers per [POL-19 Section 3.6](../02-policies/POL-19-supplier-security-policy.md). Co-sourcing arrangements preserve Internal Audit's independence and authority.

External assurance (ISO 27001 surveillance, BNM examination, NACSA examination) is **distinct from** internal audit but coordinated to avoid duplication and ensure efficient evidence gathering.
