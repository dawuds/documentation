## 1. Purpose
Specify mandatory contract clauses GIBB requires in IT outsourcing and TPSP agreements.

## 2. Scope
All material IT outsourcing contracts. Non-material agreements use a baseline subset.

## 3. Required clauses

### 3.1 Confidentiality and information protection
Confidentiality obligations binding TPSP and its sub-contractors. Information classification handling per GIBB scheme. Return / destruction of GIBB data on termination.

### 3.2 Information security
TPSP cyber controls — minimum: ISO 27001 certification (or equivalent attestation), incident notification within 24 hours of TPSP detection of incident affecting GIBB data, security testing evidence, vulnerability remediation per agreed SLA.

### 3.3 Access control
TPSP access to GIBB systems limited to authorised individuals; access logged; revoked on personnel changes; least-privilege; MFA per [STD-AC-01](STD-AC-01-password-and-authentication-standard.md) for any privileged access.

### 3.4 Sub-contracting (4th parties)
Disclosure of all sub-contractors; flow-down of GIBB security obligations to sub-contractors; GIBB notification + consent for material sub-contracting changes.

### 3.5 Right to audit
GIBB or its appointed agent may audit TPSP's compliance with the contract, including on-site (for material). BNM and (where NCII) NACSA may exercise right to audit on GIBB's behalf or independently per RMiT 10.46.

### 3.6 Service levels
SLA targets per service category; reporting cadence; remedies for breach.

### 3.7 Business continuity
TPSP maintains BC capability proportionate to GIBB criticality; BC attestation provided annually; tested per TPSP DR programme.

### 3.8 Data residency
Data residency commitments per [STD-CL-03](STD-CL-03-cloud-data-residency-standard.md); no relocation of data without GIBB notification + consent.

### 3.9 Exit assistance
Cooperation in transition to alternate provider or repatriation; data return / destruction; knowledge transfer; minimum transition period of 6 months (or longer for material).

### 3.10 Regulatory cooperation
TPSP cooperates with BNM, NACSA, and other regulator requests for information about services provided to GIBB.

### 3.11 Liability and insurance
TPSP carries minimum cyber insurance per agreed coverage; liability provisions for security and operational failures.

### 3.12 Compliance with law
TPSP complies with all laws applicable to its delivery of services to GIBB, including PDPA where personal data is processed.

### 3.13 Shariah (where Islamic product systems)
TPSP acknowledges and supports GIBB's Shariah governance; Shariah Committee oversight rights; sub-contracting of Shariah-relevant work requires consent.

## 4. Exceptions
Per [POL-10](../02-policies/POL-10-it-vendor-management-policy.md). Material contractual departures require Legal + CRO approval.
