## 1. Purpose

To define how General Bank identifies, maintains, and demonstrates compliance with legal, regulatory, contractual, and internal-policy obligations relating to information security, technology, and data.

## 2. Scope

All laws, regulations, contractual obligations, and internal policies, standards, and procedures applicable to General Bank's information and technology activities.

## 3. Policy statements

### 3.1 Identification and maintenance of obligations

- **3.1.1** The Chief Compliance Officer shall maintain a register of all applicable legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations relating to information security and technology, with each obligation mapped to the internal control(s) that implement it. *(Implements ISO/IEC 27002:2022 control 5.31.)*
- **3.1.2** The register shall be updated within 30 days of any material regulatory change, new regulation, or contractual change.

### 3.2 Records protection

- **3.2.1** Records subject to retention requirements (regulatory, legal, contractual) shall be retained, protected from unauthorised modification or destruction, and available for inspection within the timelines required by the relevant obligation. *(Implements ISO/IEC 27002:2022 control 5.33.)*

### 3.3 Privacy and personal data

- **3.3.1** Processing of personal data shall comply with the Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (Malaysia) and, where applicable, foreign data protection law. The Data Protection Officer shall maintain the Records of Processing Activity. *(Implements ISO/IEC 27002:2022 control 5.34.)*

### 3.4 Intellectual property

- **3.4.1** Use of software, content, and data shall comply with applicable intellectual property rights. Open-source software use shall be governed by an open-source software policy aligned with [POL-12 Secure Development Policy](12-secure-development-policy.md). *(Implements ISO/IEC 27002:2022 control 5.32.)*

### 3.5 Independent review

- **3.5.1** The information security function and the ISMS shall be subject to **independent review** at planned intervals. Internal Audit conducts the recurring independent review at a frequency set by the audit plan, per BNM RMiT Section 13 (Technology Audits); external assurance (certification audit, regulator-led review, independent assurance reviews per RMiT Section 14 External Party Assurance) supplements internal audit. The bank shall conduct periodic **assessment and gap analysis** against RMiT requirements per Section 18. *(Implements ISO/IEC 27002:2022 control 5.35; BNM RMiT Section 13 (Technology Audits); Section 14 (External Party Assurance); Section 18 (Assessment and Gap Analysis).)*

### 3.6 Compliance assessment

- **3.6.1** Compliance with information security policies, standards, and procedures shall be assessed through a combination of automated control monitoring, control self-assessment by 1st-line owners, 2nd-line review, and 3rd-line audit. Findings shall be tracked to remediation. *(Implements ISO/IEC 27002:2022 control 5.36.)*

### 3.7 Regulator engagement

- **3.7.1** The Chief Compliance Officer shall be the primary point of contact with Bank Negara Malaysia for technology and information security regulatory matters, coordinating closely with the CISO. Notifications under [POL-08 Incident Management Policy](08-incident-management-policy.md) Section 4.4.3 (per BNM RMiT Section 11.18) are made on this channel.
- **3.7.2** Where the bank is designated as a **National Critical Information Infrastructure (NCII)** entity under the **Cyber Security Act 2024**, the Chief Compliance Officer (jointly with the CISO) is responsible for ensuring compliance with all NACSA directives applicable to NCII entities, including any reporting obligations distinct from BNM reporting. *(BNM RMiT Section 11.4.)*

## 5. Exceptions

Per [POL-00](00-information-security-policy.md). Compliance with regulatory obligations is **not subject to exception** — only the path to compliance may vary.

## 6. Enforcement

Per [POL-00](00-information-security-policy.md). Wilful non-compliance with regulatory obligations is treated as a material disciplinary matter.
