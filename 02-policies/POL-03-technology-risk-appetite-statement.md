## 1. Purpose

The Board's annual statement of technology risk appetite — the aggregate level and types of technology risk GIBB is willing to accept in pursuit of its strategic objectives. This statement is the foundation for risk treatment decisions across the [TRMF](../01-frameworks/TRMF.md) and its cascading frameworks.

## 2. Scope

Technology risk across all GIBB business lines and functions.

## 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Risk appetite** | The aggregate level and types of technology risk the Board is willing to accept. |
| **Risk capacity** | The maximum amount of technology risk GIBB can sustain without breaching regulatory, contractual, or operational constraints. Appetite < Capacity. |
| **Risk tolerance** | The acceptable variation around a specific risk-appetite statement. |

## 4. Appetite statements

### 4.1 Overarching appetite

GIBB has a **low appetite** for technology risk that could materially affect:
- Customer trust and customer outcomes
- Regulatory standing with BNM, NACSA, the PDPA Commissioner, the Shariah Committee
- Continuity of critical services
- Shariah-compliance of product systems
- The bank's reputation

GIBB has a **moderate appetite** for technology risk taken in pursuit of:
- Strategic innovation (subject to robust risk assessment per [AIGF](../01-frameworks/AIGF.md) and TRMF)
- Operational efficiency where business case is clear and residual risk is within tolerance

### 4.2 Quantitative tolerances (illustrative — set annually)

| Risk category | Appetite | Tolerance threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Customer data confidentiality | Zero appetite for material breach | Zero confirmed breaches per year |
| Critical service availability | Low | ≥ 99.9% per RMiT-aligned classification |
| Material cyber incident (SEV-1) | Zero appetite | Zero per year |
| Material cyber incident (SEV-2) | Low | ≤ 2 per year |
| Material outsourcing failure | Zero appetite | Zero per year |
| Material Shariah non-compliance via technology | Zero appetite | Zero per year |
| Regulatory finding with material remediation | Low | ≤ 3 per BNM/NACSA examination cycle |
| End-of-support systems in production (internet-facing) | Zero appetite | Zero |
| NCII Code of Practice non-conformance | Zero appetite | Zero open beyond agreed remediation |

### 4.3 Appetite operationalisation

- **4.3.1** Risk treatment decisions **shall** target residual risk within the appetite statements above.
- **4.3.2** Residual risk above appetite **shall** require acceptance per the TRMF authority matrix or treatment to bring within appetite.
- **4.3.3** Sustained breach of tolerance **shall** be escalated to RMC and (for material categories) to the Board.
