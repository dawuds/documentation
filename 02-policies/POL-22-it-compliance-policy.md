## 1. Purpose

To define how GIBB identifies, maintains, evidences, and demonstrates compliance with the legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations applicable to its IT and information processing. The policy is the bank's IT-compliance umbrella, working in concert with the dedicated frameworks for NCII (NCIIF) and customer information (CIMF), and the broader Compliance Management Framework owned by the CCO.

## 2. Scope

All applicable laws, regulations, contractual obligations, and internal policies/standards/procedures relating to IT and information processing at GIBB.

## 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Compliance obligation** | A specific legal, regulatory, or contractual requirement applicable to GIBB. |
| **Control objective** | The specific outcome a control is designed to achieve in satisfying a compliance obligation. |
| **Compliance attestation** | A formal statement of compliance against a defined obligation. |
| **Gap analysis** | Structured assessment of current state versus required state, per RMiT Section 18. |

## 4. Policy statements

### 4.1 Identification and maintenance of obligations

- **4.1.1** The CCO **shall** maintain a register of all applicable IT-related legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations, mapped to internal controls implementing each. *(Implements ISO/IEC 27002:2022 control 5.31.)*
- **4.1.2** The register **shall** be updated within 30 days of any material regulatory change.

### 4.2 Records protection

- **4.2.1** Records subject to retention requirements **shall** be retained, protected, and available within statutory timelines. *(Implements ISO/IEC 27002:2022 control 5.33.)*

### 4.3 Privacy

- **4.3.1** Personal data processing **shall** comply with PDPA 2010 per [CIMF](../01-frameworks/CIMF.md). *(Implements ISO/IEC 27002:2022 control 5.34.)*

### 4.4 Intellectual property

- **4.4.1** Use of software, content, and data **shall** comply with applicable intellectual property rights. Open-source software use **shall** be governed by an open-source software policy aligned with [POL-17](POL-17-secure-development-policy.md). *(Implements ISO/IEC 27002:2022 control 5.32.)*

### 4.5 Technology audits

- **4.5.1** The IT function and the cascading frameworks **shall** be subject to **technology audit** per RMiT Section 13, by Internal Audit on a planned cycle, supplemented by external assurance where mandated. *(Implements RMiT 13.1–13.4; ISO/IEC 27002:2022 control 5.35.)*

### 4.6 External party assurance

- **4.6.1** Where required, external party assurance **shall** be obtained per RMiT Section 14 (e.g., for material outsourcing arrangements; for digital service enhancements per RMiT Section 16.4).

### 4.7 Compliance with internal policies, standards, procedures

- **4.7.1** Compliance with internal policies, standards, and procedures **shall** be assessed through automated control monitoring, 1st-line control self-assessment, 2nd-line review, and 3rd-line audit. Findings **shall** be tracked to remediation. *(Implements ISO/IEC 27002:2022 control 5.36.)*

### 4.8 Assessment and gap analysis

- **4.8.1** GIBB **shall** conduct **periodic assessment and gap analysis** against RMiT requirements per RMiT Section 18, with remediation tracked through the Corrective Action Register.

### 4.9 Regulator engagement

- **4.9.1** The CCO **shall** be the primary point of contact with BNM for technology and information security regulatory matters, coordinating closely with the CISO. NACSA engagement is per [NCIIF](../01-frameworks/NCIIF.md).

## 6. Exceptions

Compliance with regulatory obligations is **not subject to exception** within GIBB authority — only the path to compliance may vary.

## 7. Enforcement

Per TRMF Section 12. Wilful non-compliance with regulatory obligations is treated as a material disciplinary matter.
