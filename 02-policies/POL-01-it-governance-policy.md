## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the governance of information technology at General Islamic Bank Berhad (GIBB). It defines how the Board, executive management, and operating functions direct, oversee, and monitor IT — ensuring IT investments deliver value, technology risks are managed within appetite, and IT operates in service of business strategy. The policy is the master IT-governance principle document under the [Technology Risk Management Framework (TRMF)](../01-frameworks/TRMF.md).

## 2. Scope

**Applies to:** all IT-related decisions of GIBB — strategy, investment, architecture, sourcing, operations, risk, and oversight. All personnel involved in IT decision-making — Board, executive committees, IT leadership, business leadership, operating staff, third parties to whom IT functions are delegated.

**Does not apply to:** pure business strategy decisions without IT implications (covered by enterprise strategy governance).

## 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **IT governance** | The system by which the use of information technology is directed and controlled — to ensure IT contributes to GIBB strategy, manages risks within appetite, and uses resources responsibly. |
| **Board-level Technology Committee** | The Board sub-committee responsible for ongoing oversight of technology and cyber risk per RMiT 8.3. |
| **I&T Management Framework** | The integrated governance and management framework for information and technology, per COBIT 2019 APO01. |

## 4. Policy statements

### 4.1 Board oversight of IT

- **4.1.1** The Board **shall** approve the bank's **technology risk appetite** annually. *(Implements RMiT Section 8.1; COBIT EDM03.)*
- **4.1.2** The Board **shall** maintain ongoing oversight responsibilities over technology and cyber risk through the Board Risk Management Committee. *(Implements RMiT Section 8.2.)*
- **4.1.3** The Board **shall** establish a **Board-level Technology Committee** (which at GIBB is discharged by the Board Risk Management Committee with a dedicated technology agenda) per RMiT 8.3.
- **4.1.4** The Board Audit Committee **shall** receive technology-related Internal Audit reports per RMiT 8.5.
- **4.1.5** Technology and cyber risk **shall** be a standing agenda item at the Board level no less than annually, and at the Board Risk Management Committee no less than quarterly. *(Implements RMiT 8.4.)*

### 4.2 Senior management accountability

- **4.2.1** The CEO **shall** be accountable to the Board for the execution of technology strategy and the management of technology risk within Board-approved appetite. *(Implements RMiT 8.6.)*
- **4.2.2** A **cross-functional Technology Committee** at executive level **shall** oversee day-to-day technology governance, with membership including CIO, CISO, CRO, COO, CCO, and CDO. *(Implements RMiT 8.7.)*

### 4.3 IT governance framework

- **4.3.1** GIBB **shall** maintain an I&T Management Framework (this IT Governance Policy plus the [TRMF](../01-frameworks/TRMF.md) and supporting frameworks) covering strategy, investment, architecture, value, risk, and performance. *(Implements COBIT APO01; ISO/IEC 38500.)*
- **4.3.2** The IT governance framework **shall** integrate with the Enterprise Risk Management Framework per RMiT 9.1.

### 4.4 IT strategy and value

- **4.4.1** IT strategy **shall** align with and enable GIBB's business strategy. The CIO **shall** maintain a multi-year IT strategy approved annually. *(Implements COBIT APO02.)*
- **4.4.2** IT investment **shall** be governed through a portfolio process with documented value cases, risk assessment, and post-implementation benefit realisation. *(Implements COBIT EDM02; APO05; APO06.)*

### 4.5 Independent technology risk function

- **4.5.1** GIBB **shall** maintain an independent Technology Risk Management function in the second line of defence per RMiT 9.3, operating per the [TRMF](../01-frameworks/TRMF.md).

### 4.6 Designated CISO

- **4.6.1** GIBB **shall** designate a Chief Information Security Officer per RMiT 9.4 with responsibilities per RMiT 9.5. The CISO is functionally accountable to the CRO and operationally independent of the CIO.

### 4.7 Shariah considerations in IT governance

- **4.7.1** IT decisions affecting Islamic finance product systems **shall** be subject to Shariah review per the BNM Shariah Governance Framework. The Shariah Committee approves new product-system implementations and material changes before deployment.
