## Policy statement

**General Islamic Bank Berhad (GIBB)** is committed to protecting the confidentiality, integrity, availability, and authenticity of information entrusted to it by customers, employees, regulators, business partners, and the public — recognising that information security underpins customer trust, the bank's licence to operate, and the bank's role as a designated National Critical Information Infrastructure entity in the Malaysian financial sector.

To discharge this commitment the Board of Directors and Senior Management of GIBB:

1. **Establish and maintain an Information Security Management System** that conforms to **ISO/IEC 27001:2022** and meets the expectations of **Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM RMiT, 28 November 2025)**, the **Cyber Security Act 2024 and NACSA Code of Practice**, the **Personal Data Protection Act 2010**, the **Financial Services Act 2013 / Islamic Financial Services Act 2013**, the **BNM Management of Customer Information and Permitted Disclosures policy**, and the **BNM Shariah Governance Framework** as it bears on technology.

2. **Set, monitor, and review** measurable information security objectives — recorded in [REG-OBJ](../06-registers/REG-OBJ-information-security-objectives-register.md) — and resource the ISMS adequately to achieve them.

3. **Operate the ISMS within a stated risk appetite** — approved by the Board through [POL-03 Technology Risk Appetite Statement](POL-03-technology-risk-appetite-statement.md) — and ensure that residual risk is accepted only at the authority commensurate with its rating.

4. **Comply with all applicable legal, regulatory, contractual, and Shariah obligations** relating to information security and customer information protection.

5. **Continually improve** the ISMS, drawing on incidents, audit findings, regulatory feedback, technological change, and the changing threat landscape — formalised through the annual Management Review per [REG-MR](../06-registers/REG-MR-management-review-register.md) and through continuous improvement under ISO 27001 Clause 10.

6. **Build and sustain** information security awareness and competence across the workforce — and require equivalent commitment from outsourced service providers — under [PLN-07 Awareness and Competence Programme](../05-plans/PLN-07-awareness-and-competence-programme.md).

7. **Maintain readiness** to detect, respond to, recover from, and notify regulators of cyber incidents within applicable timeframes — operationalised through [PLN-01 Incident Response Plan](../05-plans/PLN-01-incident-response-plan.md).

8. **Hold senior management and accountable role-holders responsible** for the operation of controls under the framework — and provide the resources, authority, and information needed for them to discharge that responsibility.

## Authority

This policy statement is adopted by the Board of Directors. The CISO is accountable for the operation of the Information Security Management System; the CRO oversees second-line independence; the Chief Internal Auditor provides third-line independent assurance to the Board Audit Committee per [PLN-08](../05-plans/PLN-08-internal-audit-plan.md).
