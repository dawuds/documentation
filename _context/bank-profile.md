## Governance structure

### Board level
- Board of Directors
- Board Risk Management Committee
- Board Audit Committee
- **Shariah Committee** (Islamic bank-specific; reports directly to the Board per BNM SGF)

### Executive level
- CEO
- Chief Operating Officer (COO)
- Chief Financial Officer (CFO)
- Chief Risk Officer (CRO)
- Chief Compliance Officer (CCO)
- Chief Information Officer (CIO)
- Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)
- Chief Data Officer (CDO)
- Head of Internal Audit (reports to Board Audit Committee)
- Head of Shariah Compliance (functional report to Shariah Committee)

### Three-line model (federated GRC)

| Line | Functions |
|---|---|
| **1st line** | IT, business units, operations — operate controls |
| **2nd line** | Technology Risk (under CRO), Compliance (under CCO), Shariah Compliance (under Shariah Committee) — challenge and oversee |
| **3rd line** | Internal Audit (under Board Audit Committee); Shariah Audit (parallel track for Shariah matters) |

## Current state of IT governance (the starting baseline for v2)

- **Policies exist** — accreted over years, mixed authorship, inconsistent voice
- **Procedures exist** — varying maturity by function; some duplicated, some missing
- **Frameworks** — no consistent framework documents; tech risk and cyber risk are addressed in scattered policies rather than in unified TRMF / CRMF
- **Registers** — operational registers exist (incident, risk, vendor) but not consistently maintained
- **Document control** — patchy; no consistent versioning, approval tracking, or supersession discipline
- **Regulatory coverage** — BNM RMiT (2020 issuance) addressed in older policies; not yet refreshed for the 28 Nov 2025 issuance
- **Shariah governance** — separate track, run by Shariah Committee; touchpoints with IT for product systems but no formal IT/Shariah integration
- **NCII compliance** — designation acknowledged; NACSA Code of Practice implementation in progress

The v2 blueprint is the **target architecture** that will rationalise this baseline.
